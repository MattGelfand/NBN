NbN Styleguide

Overview

North by Northwestern is the university's leading independent online publication, updated with news and stories about campus and culture. Founded in 2006, it began as strictly a paper magazine, and made the jump to online in 2009.

This project entailed designing new concepts for NbN's article and homepage, addressing as many of the needs of the site's editors, contributors and readers as possible. Common feedback included dissatisfaction with the clutter of the homepage, as there are too many boxes situated too far down the page. Article pages were bland and did not provide a clear avenue for readers to find related content. The site was also not responsive, an issue that needed to be addressed. Other needs included chronological sorting tools to easier display the history of the publication, a better way to access old stories and generally upping the "cool"factor.

Branding

Branding will be addressed by stakeholders, as new logo designs promised to the designer never materialized. Determination of branding types ("N" logo and "North by Northwestern" spelled out) and their placement would have been personal preference of the designer, and thus seems like a decision process more suited for the stakeholders at a future date.
